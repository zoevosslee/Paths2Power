About Paths to Power

Paths to Power is a collaborative project funded by the African American Action Fund ‘Places of Black Women Activism’ grant centered on spaces of Black resistance, community-building, and joy in Civil Rights-era.  With Paths to Power, we honor the paths and places of this enduring legacy by mapping the spaces that ignited radical change and collective empowerment during the Civil Rights era. These are the places where Black women stood tall, built communities, created joy, and fought tirelessly for justice—shaping the very fabric of our society.

Our pilot project focuses on Brooklyn, spotlighting the often-overlooked yet powerful stories of Black women’s activism. Through a collaborative effort funded by the African American Action Fund’s Places of Black Women Activism grant, we are preserving the spaces where critical movements or events took place.

Partnering with historic institutions and drawing on crowd-sourced narratives, we weave together places that inhabit the political memory, scholarship, and leadership of Black women who’ve redefined what it means to resist. From organizing hubs to cultural sanctuaries, these places reflect the strength, vision, and unapologetic power of Black women as agents of radical change.

Our Presence is Power, then and now.
